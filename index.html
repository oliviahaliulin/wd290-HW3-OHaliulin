The Impressionist Revolution: Monet to Matisse

A bold break from academic tradition forever reshaped the future of art. Artists turned away from rigid rules and embraced color, light, and movement as living forces, capturing fleeting moments and raw emotion. Discover how this radical shift transformed modern painting and changed the way we see the world.

In 1874, a group of artists rejected rigid academic standards in favor of capturing fleeting impressions of modern life.

Loose brushwork, unconventional composition, and vibrant color reshaped how perception could be painted.

Impressionism laid the groundwork for modern art in the twentieth century.

“To paint not what you see, but what you feel.” - Edgar Degas
